Honours in Social Anthropology

During my undergraduate at Dalhousie University, my love for social anthropology & Latin American studies led me to study abroad in Peru to live with a host family and work for a non-profit travel organization.

Upon returning, I explored how Indigenous studies, philosophy, and social anthropology intersected in my honours thesis on how different academic disciplinary areas perceive the facilitators and barriers of integrating diverse perspectives into curricula at Dalhousie.

Social Purpose

Having been involved with organizations like Make-A-Wish, Right to Play, Boys & Girl’s Club, and Frontier College, coming out of university and back to Toronto, I leapt into the world of tech startups to leverage my anthropology skills to create a social impact.

Email Marketer

As an email marketer at CareGuide (a tech startup in Toronto that helps match caregivers and those needing care), I gained experience in a broad range of skills including copy-writing, design, development & data analysis while advancing our customer engagement metrics and creating processes to improve the efficacy of email creation & iteration.

Designer & Researcher

Eager to make a larger impact on how people experience our products, I moved to Jr. Product Manager. I started user research projects to better understand how our value propositions were resonating with our customers. Taking those learnings, I collaborated with engineering to design, implement, & measure changes to our value proposition.

Product Manager

I’m now the product manager of Nanny Lane at CareGuide, where I actively conduct user research, analyze data & lead a team of designers & engineers to drive value and product growth.
